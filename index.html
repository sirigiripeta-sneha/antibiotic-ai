THE PROBLEM

The Growing Threat of Antibiotic Resistance

Antibiotic resistance occurs when bacteria evolve to survive the drugs designed to kill them. Every year, at least 1.27 million deaths worldwide are directly attributed to drug-resistant infections — more than HIV or malaria.

Without action, resistant infections could claim 10 million lives annually by 2050, costing the global economy $100 trillion. Current surveillance systems are slow, fragmented, and unable to predict emerging threats.
